## About Group Discussion

Group Discussion is a common round in IBM's recruitment process to assess communication skills, leadership, team collaboration, and analytical thinking.

---

## GD Evaluation Criteria

| Criteria | Weightage | What Evaluators Look For |
|----------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Communication** | 25% | Clarity, fluency, vocabulary |
| **Content** | 25% | Quality of points, facts, examples |
| **Leadership** | 20% | Initiative, facilitation, conclusion |
| **Teamwork** | 15% | Listening, building on others' points |
| **Body Language** | 15% | Eye contact, gestures, confidence |

---

## GD Do's âœ…

1. **Enter the discussion early** - Don't wait too long to speak
2. **Listen actively** - Build on others' points
3. **Maintain eye contact** - With all participants, not just panelists
4. **Use facts and examples** - Support your arguments with data
5. **Be assertive, not aggressive** - Respect different opinions
6. **Summarize if possible** - Shows leadership
7. **Use proper hand gestures** - Controlled, open movements
8. **Speak clearly and audibly** - Moderate pace

---

## GD Don'ts âŒ

1. **Don't interrupt others** - Wait for natural pauses
2. **Don't dominate** - Give others chance to speak
3. **Don't get personal** - Attack ideas, not people
4. **Don't stay silent** - Minimum 3-4 quality contributions
5. **Don't repeat the same point** - Add new perspectives
6. **Don't lose temper** - Stay calm under disagreement
7. **Don't look at ceiling/floor** - Maintain eye contact
8. **Don't use slang** - Use formal language

---

## Useful Phrases for GD

### Starting the Discussion

- "Let's begin by understanding the topic..."
- "I'd like to initiate the discussion by stating that..."
- "To set the context, I would say..."

### Adding a Point

- "Building on what [Name] said..."
- "I'd like to add another perspective..."
- "Additionally, we should consider..."

### Disagreeing Politely

- "I see your point, however..."
- "That's an interesting perspective, but I believe..."
- "While I understand your view, I'd like to offer an alternative..."

### Bringing Back on Topic

- "These are valid points, but let's focus on..."
- "To get back to the main topic..."

### Summarizing

- "To summarize our discussion..."
- "The key points discussed were..."
- "In conclusion, we can say that..."

## Sample GD Topic Analysis

### Topic: "AI Replacing Human Jobs - Boon or Bane?"

**Points FOR AI:**

- Automates repetitive, mundane tasks
- Increases efficiency and productivity
- Creates new job categories (AI trainers, ethicists)
- Handles dangerous tasks (mining, nuclear)
- Available 24/7, no fatigue
- Example: IBM Watson in healthcare diagnosis

**Points AGAINST AI:**

- Mass unemployment in certain sectors
- Loss of human touch in services
- High implementation cost for SMEs
- Privacy and security concerns
- Skill gap - workers need retraining
- Example: Factory automation reducing labor

**Balanced Conclusion:**
> "AI is neither purely a boon nor a bane - it's a tool. The key lies in how we adapt. Rather than fearing job loss, we should focus on upskilling and creating policies for a smooth transition. History shows that every technological revolution created more jobs than it destroyed. The same can be true for AI if we prepare wisely."

## Case Study GD (IBM Specific)

IBM may also conduct case-study based GDs where you analyze a business scenario.

### Sample Case
>
> "A startup has developed an AI-based recruitment tool. However, studies show it has unconscious bias against certain demographics. As a consulting team, discuss whether the company should launch it."

**Approach:**

1. Understand the ethical dilemma
2. Consider stakeholders (company, candidates, society)
3. Analyze pros and cons of each option
4. Propose solutions (bias audits, diverse training data)
5. Recommend a balanced approach
